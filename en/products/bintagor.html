BINTANGOR FACED PLYWOOD

Bintangor-faced plywood is a widely used material among furniture manufacturers, packaging factories, and wood product producers. With competitive pricing, consistent color, and easy surface processing, it delivers a balanced combination of cost efficiency, aesthetics, and durability, making it ideal for large-volume and cost-sensitive markets.

1. Surface Characteristics of Bintangor Plywood

Bintangor veneer features a natural reddish-brown tone with a light, subtle grain, creating an attractive finish when painted or PU-coated. The surface is highly workable and does not require extensive preparation, helping manufacturers save both processing time and production costs.

In addition, Bintangor plywood has a moderate weight, is easy to cut and assemble, and causes less wear on cutting tools compared to harder wood species.

2. Key Advantages of Bintangor Plywood in Manufacturing

One of the main strengths of Bintangor plywood is its versatility. It can be produced to meet various bonding and durability standards—from medium-grade glue for packaging applications to higher-grade glue for interior furniture use.

When manufactured with well-dried acacia or eucalyptus core veneers, Bintangor plywood demonstrates good dimensional stability, minimal warping, and a low surface defect rate, ensuring reliable performance in mass production.

3. Why Choose Bintangor Plywood for Your Business?

  • - Competitive pricing, ideal for large-scale production
  • - Easy processing, enabling faster manufacturing cycles
  • - Stable aesthetics, easy to paint and color-match
  • - Abundant and reliable supply, suitable for container orders
  • - An ideal solution for businesses seeking stable quality, cost efficiency, and scalable production
