Is Film Faced Plywood Suitable for Mezzanine Flooring?

15/08/2026 • VietPlywood

Film faced plywood for mezzanine, low-cost mezzanine flooring, plywood vs Cemboard comparison, fast mezzanine construction

1. The Growing Demand for Affordable and Fast Mezzanine Solutions

In densely populated cities or in response to the need for additional storage space in online retail shops, mezzanine floors have become an effective space-optimization solution. This raises a common question: Should you choose Cemboard (cement fiber board) or film faced plywood?

While Cemboard offers long-term durability, film faced plywood is increasingly preferred by tenants and shop owners due to its flexibility, lower upfront cost, and ease of installation and removal.

2. Key Advantages of Film Faced Plywood for Mezzanine Floors

  • Lightweight Structure: An 18mm film faced plywood sheet weighs approximately 35 kg, whereas a Cemboard panel of the same size can weigh nearly twice as much. This significantly reduces the load on the steel frame, allowing the use of smaller steel sections and lowering total structural costs.
  • Clean and Fast Installation: Cutting plywood does not generate fine, harmful dust like cement boards do. No mortar, no curing time—once installed, the space can be used immediately.
  • Aesthetic and Warm Appearance: With a smooth black or brown film surface, film faced plywood serves as a clean, finished surface that is easy to maintain. Compared to the cold feel of cement, wood-based panels create a warmer and more comfortable environment.

3. Limitations and Practical Solutions

The main drawbacks of plywood are lower water resistance compared to cement boards and a degree of elastic deflection.

Reducing Floor Deflection: For mezzanines used for living or heavy storage, it is recommended to:

  • Use thicker steel framing (joist spacing around 40 cm × 40 cm).
  • Select plywood with a minimum thickness of 18mm.

For higher performance, installing two staggered plywood layers can completely eliminate vibration when walking.

Moisture Protection: In bathrooms or cooking areas, all cut edges and panel joints must be sealed with silicone or waterproof sealant to prevent moisture penetration and mold formation.

4. When Is Film Faced Plywood the Right Choice for Mezzanines?

Film faced plywood is the top choice in the following scenarios:

  • Rental houses and retail shops: Fast installation, low cost, and—most importantly—easy dismantling and reuse when returning the property. Cemboard panels often break during removal, while plywood panels remain reusable.
  • Dry goods storage: Clothing, footwear, and general merchandise warehouses benefit from a cost-effective and practical mezzanine solution.
  • Temporary mezzanines: Worker housing, site offices, and temporary construction facilities.
